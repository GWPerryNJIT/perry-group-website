II. CURRENT RESEARCH INTERESTS AND FUTURE PLANS

My research group’s scientific interests are currently centered on the plasma

dynamics of the near-Earth geospace system and particularly in the terrestrial high-

latitude ionosphere. To accomplish this, we use remote sensing (e.g., radars, radio

receivers, etc.), numerical modeling, and in situ (e.g., satellites) techniques to

characterize the state of the terrestrial ionosphere and specify the complex plasma

processes underway there. Societal applications of this work include the

identification of space weather hazards.

Members of my research group are currently involved in several projects and

collaborations, including a project studying how a solar eclipse can affect the

ionosphere, characterizing and modeling power and Doppler fluctuations observed

by high-frequency radar systems (due to space weather effects) at polar latitudes,

identifying agents of space weather in radar data, and developing and validating

techniques to identify intense plasma dynamics in spacecraft data. Many of these

projects are multiyear efforts and, therefore, will be the target of the bulk of the

group’s research activities for the next few years.

The current composition of my research group is two postdoctoral fellows, one PhD

student, and one MSc-level analyst.
